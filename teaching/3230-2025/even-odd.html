EVEN AND ODD PERMUTATIONS

This is a short note to describe explicitly which elements of S4 (the symmetric group on 4 elements) and D4 (the symmetries of a square) are even and which are odd. Recall that we can write every permutation σ as a product of transpositions; then σ is even if it is the product of an even number of transpositions, and odd if it is the product of an odd number of transpositions.

2. S4

The symmetric group S4 has 4! = 24 permutations, and we saw in class that half of them are even and half are odd. So we are looking for 12 even permutations (the elements of A4) and 12 odd permutations.

As we saw in lecture, the identity element e is an even permutation.
